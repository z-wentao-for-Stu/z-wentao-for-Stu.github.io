Title: UAM Vertiport Location Decision Based on Network Connectivity

This thesis tackles vertiport siting in Urban Air Mobility by coupling network connectivity, battery range, and passenger accessibility. A connectivityâ€‘aware location model (construction cost, accessibility, range) is solved via CPLEX; a hybrid GA in MATLAB, Python analytics, and ArcGIS Pro visualization support experiments. On synthetic networks and a New York City case, connectivity is ensured, system cost is reduced, and accessibility improves versus baselines. Sensitivity analysis shows how range and facility count shape network structure and cost.
