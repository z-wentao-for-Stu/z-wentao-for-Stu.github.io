Master's Thesis

Title: UAM Vertiport Location Decision Based on Network Connectivity

This thesis addresses the siting of vertiports in Urban Air Mobility (UAM) networks, overcoming limitations in existing studies that neglect the coupling of network connectivity, battery range, and passenger accessibility. It proposes a location optimization model considering connectivity, construction costs, accessibility, and flight range constraints. The study uses CPLEX for mathematical programming, a hybrid genetic algorithm in MATLAB, Python-based data analysis, and ArcGIS Pro for spatial visualization. Simulations on synthetic networks and a real-world New York City case demonstrate improved connectivity, lower system costs, and enhanced accessibility. Sensitivity analysis further highlights the impact of range limits and facility count on network structure and cost.

Journal Articles

  • Zhang, W., & Hwang, T. (2025). UAM Vertiport Network Design Considering Connectivity. Systems, 13(7), 607. (SSCI Q1, IF=3.1, First Author)
    Problem: Traditional facility location models ignore network structure constraints in UAM.
    Method: Proposed a location model with connectivity constraints and developed a hybrid MST-GA+GI algorithm, using greedy initialization to enhance stability.
    Result: On synthetic and real NYC datasets (444 candidates), the model reduced cost and improved convergence, proving superiority over baseline methods.
    Contribution: First to systematically integrate connectivity in UAM siting, proposing a scalable framework for real-world deployments.
  • Xu, S., & Zhang, W. (2025). An Improved DOA for Global Optimization and Cloud Task Scheduling. Symmetry, 17. (SCI Q2, IF=2.2, Second Author)
    Proposed IDOA (Improved Dhole Optimization Algorithm) with Sobol initialization, sine elite search, and random mirror boundary strategies. Achieved better performance on CEC2017 benchmarks and cloud task scheduling scenarios, reducing makespan by ~12% and energy by ~15%.
